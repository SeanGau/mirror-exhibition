Concept

The vinyl record is a carrier of acceptance and release. I imagine the melody by looking at the tracks on the record, composing the rhythm of the week in my head. The melody was extended from the idea that a spinning thread flows into the heart. The tracks respectively are: Artist's Life, Sell My Soul for You, The Night of the South, Trends of Thought, The Flow of Dreams & Travelers of Reminiscence, Deep in the Dark, and What Will You Do on Sunday. The tracks were chosen to interpret states, such as interiority, mind, period, cycle, etc. Through weaving, embroidery, winding, etc., I make colors, light, fibers and materials meet, letting these elements overlap, intertwine, communicate and flow with each other. The moment I put down a record, I activate my consciousness. Just like blood circulating in my brain and body, senses such as sight, hearing, touch, etc. are once again awakened. The record that carries the artist's consciousness could then continue playing. This is a song about cranial nerves, perception, silence, meditation and rumination.

Artist

KANG Ya-Chu‚ÄßTextile Map

KANG Ya-Chu is a cross-disciplinary artist working with multi-media. She takes the tracks of fibers and weaving threads as a path to thought, navigating and locating herself through field works, archival materials, and raised propositions. She then looks back at the particularity and difference of her own cultural background to understand the present moment she settles in. In her works, Kang questions topics such as globalization and environmental issues, and explores the relationship between craftsmanship and physical labor, living conditions, migration, economic trade and social structure. She also examines the alternation, interweaving and tensions between global and local cultures by taking the history of global textile culture and the representation of contemporary social behavior as a foundation. Kang's works range from sculptures, site specific installations, environmental art, textiles, and paintings to sound videos.
