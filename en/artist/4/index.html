In response to the exhibition concept â€œhealing,â€ I thought of the â€œlightâ€ of the night lamp: a light that creates a situational atmosphere, or gives a little direction in the dark, rather than lighting. I remember that when I was a child, there was a fiber optic light at home â€” a slender bundle of optical fiber was inserted into the spherical base. The optical fiber dangles and spreads out naturally. The top of each optical fiber was illuminated due to the light source transmitted from the base. The whole thing looks like a starry sky. Although the light was made with a very simple scientific principle and a minimum of technological skill, the visual experience it creates fascinates me so much that I can stare at it for a long time. In this series of works, I tried to combine ceramic and light to represent such a viewing experience. Using the structure and sculpting of ceramic works, adding low-lumen light bulbs, I shape the appearance of light, and further store the light in a tiny space. The viewer is then able to see the different faces of the light from various angles.
