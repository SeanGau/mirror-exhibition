I divided the soil I collected into five stages: soil, clay, bisque-firing (a state that can still absorb water), and high-temperature firing (sintering), trying to present the sense of healing I felt during these processes. This is like the living states of earth, as well as the faces of my life.

â€œlifeâ€ was chosen as the motif of this work. I do not want to address it in a too serious way. â€œlifeâ€ refers to the process of living. The clay has a memory. It records the time and strength I use to shape and adjust it. It also records my condition at every moment. While looking at the clay in its state, I feel like I am looking at the distance I have traveled.
